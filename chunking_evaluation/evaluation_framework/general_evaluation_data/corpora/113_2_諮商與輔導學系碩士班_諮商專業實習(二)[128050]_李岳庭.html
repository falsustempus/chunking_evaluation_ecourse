課程目標   Learning Objectives
1. To become more familiar with how a counseling institution develops, organizes and processes counseling works.
2. To increase skills in doing counseling/ family therapy with assessments, individuals, groups, couples and families.
3. To understand the many ethical and professional issues which are related to doing counseling/ family therapy; to include understanding the ethical codes of Chinese Guidance and Counseling Association, American Counseling Association (ACA), American Association for Marriage and Family Therapy (AAMFT).
4. To increase ones understanding of the interfacing of the many agencies that serve individuals, groups, couples and families.
5. To further develop professional identity and refine ones professional role as a counselor/ family therapist.
6. To become more comfortable doing counseling/ therapy and in receiving feedback about ones work.
7. To become more flexible in working with individuals, groups, couples and families, i.e., to become more creative in the counseling/ therapy session.
8. To develop more mastery of your chosen model of counseling/ family therapy.
9. To more understanding of how humans change.

3. Multi-culture (多元文化)experience 20 %
4. Exam: 20%
四、作業：
1. Reading: It is expected for all readings in the textbook to be completed on time.
2. Students are expected to report a chapter in the class.
3. Schedule individual supervision with the instructor if needed.
4. Community Service(社區服務)： Each student will be asked to volunteer 50 hours per week in a school (any type), community institution, or non-profit organization…etc （例如社區及非營利組織等）by using what they have learn in the class(應用課堂所學). All the services need to be proved by the instructor before the internship starts.
5. Case Presentation/ class discussion：the student will be asked to present cases and experience they gain from their community service work in order to improve their ability of self-introspect (增進自我反思能力). Prepare a one page brief handout for each case presentation with your videotape as well as your agent tests. The tape should be cued up! No excuse for any equipment problems. Each student should make sure the audio and video of the tape is clear before he/she demonstrates his/her session in the class.
6. Keep a log of all direct and indirect services. Practicum diary is highly recommended.
7. Be prepared to identify your model of individual couple and family counseling/therapy at the beginning of each class. Be prepared to clarify its assumptions, concepts and answer questions about the model.
8. Be ready to explore yourself. Be ready to be challenged of your value and personal experience.
9. Confidentiality is required.
10. Share your multi-culture experience in the class: Each student is asked to explore two multi-culture activities and share his/her experience in the class. No paper work is needed.
