課程目標   Learning Objectives
Effective management of human resources within organizations requires an understanding of various behavior and processes. Managers need to know why people behave as they do in relation to their jobs, their work groups and their organizations. The purpose of this course is to provide students a foundation for understanding basic concepts and theoretical frameworks of organizational behavior (OB). Related implications to business practices are also included in the class discussions. Through reading assignments, term papers, and mid-term exams, students should gain an appreciation for the current status of theory and practices on OB.

The focus of instruction will move progressively through the individual, group and
organizational levels of behavior and will examine the interrelationships of behavioral phenomena among these levels. Additionally, concepts such as motivation, communication and leadership and their relevance to organizational behavior will be examined in detail. Students’ participation in class discussions is also required and encouraged.

10 Group behavior lecture&group discussion  Assigned reading: ch.9  In class discussion: discuss how group size and diversity impact individual performance and group effectiveness. 
11 Work teams lecture&group discussion  Assigned reading: ch.10  In class discussion: Explain the critical contextual factors that determine team effectiveness. 
12 Communication lecture&group discussion  Assigned reading: ch.11  In class activity: The puzzle game

From the results of this activity, what differences do you see among the groups?

This course requires thoughtful readings, active group discussions, and team work. The instructor’s main role will be to facilitate the implementations of these activities and provide constant guidance along each step of the way. The final grade will be based on the following four components.
1. Regular class participation & in class discussion & assignments(15%)
2. Quality of student’s presentation and discussion of assigned questions/book chapters(35%)
3. Mid-term exam (15%)
4. Final Term paper (35%)
